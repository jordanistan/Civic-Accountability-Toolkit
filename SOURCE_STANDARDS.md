# Source Reliability & Evidence Standards

To maintain the highest level of public trust and legal safety, all data added to this toolkit must adhere to the following standards.

## Reliability Tiers

### Tier 1: Authoritative / Official
- **Court Records:** Signed orders, filed complaints, trial transcripts.
- **Government Records:** Signed Executive Orders, agency memorandums, IG reports, GAO audits.
- **Statutes:** U.S. Code, Code of Federal Regulations, State Laws.

### Tier 2: Documented Reporting
- Major news organizations with named sources and primary document links.
- Verified whistleblower testimony with corroborating metadata.

### Tier 3: Expert Analysis
- Watchdog group reports (e.g., ACLU, CREW, Heritage Foundation).
- Letters from members of Congress or oversight committees.
- Legal analysis from recognized constitutional scholars.

### Tier 4: Public Allegations
- Commentary, opinion pieces, or reports based on anonymous sourcing without primary documentation.
- These must be clearly labeled as **"Alleged"** or **"Reported"** and should not be used as the primary basis for verified claims.

## Confidence Labels

Every claim or incident must be tagged with a confidence level:
1. **Verified:** Documented by Tier 1 sources.
2. **Strongly Documented:** Corroborated by multiple independent Tier 2 sources.
3. **Reported:** Significant reporting exists but primary documents are unavailable.
4. **Alleged:** Claim is public but evidence is contested or limited.
5. **Disputed:** Subject has issued a formal denial or there is conflicting evidence.
