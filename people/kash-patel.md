# Kash Patel

## Overview
Kash Patel has served in multiple high-level national security roles. In his current role as FBI Director, he is overseeing a broad initiative to restructure agency personnel and redirect investigative priorities.

## Documented Legal / Constitutional / Ethics Concerns

### FBI Personnel Purge (2025)
- **Date:** January 2025 – Present
- **Type:** Administrative Action / Civil Service Dispute
- **Summary:** Following his appointment, Patel initiated the removal of dozens of career FBI agents and officials, primarily those who had been involved in previous investigations related to Donald Trump.
- **Possible Issue:** Potential violation of civil service protections and whistleblower retaliation.
- **Link:** [[incidents/2025-fbi-personnel-purge|2025-fbi-personnel-purge]]

### Retribution Lawsuit (2026)
- **Date:** March 2026
- **Type:** Court Finding / Active Litigation
- **Summary:** A class-action lawsuit (*Agents v. FBI*) was filed alleging that the 2025 purges were retaliatory and denied due process to career officials.
- **Link:** [[incidents/2026-fbi-retribution-lawsuit|2026-fbi-retribution-lawsuit]]

## Linked Statutes
- **[[statutes/public-integrity-statutes|public-integrity-statutes]]**: Defines the standards for professional conduct and non-retaliation within federal agencies.
- **[[statutes/fifth-amendment-due-process|fifth-amendment-due-process]]**: Guarantees that individuals cannot be deprived of property (including certain job protections) without due process.

## Suggested Oversight Ask
Ordinary citizens may request that the **House Committee on Oversight and Accountability** review the legal justification for the mass termination of career civil servants at the FBI and ensure that the **Whistleblower Protection Act** is being fully upheld during agency restructuring.
